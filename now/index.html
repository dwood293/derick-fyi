World History: Strategy, Causes, and Reasons for Being

It's dark before I leave for work; it's dark on my drive home. For me, this cycle spurs introspection. In order to curb any SAD, this is my time to read, study, and understand.

Here's what I'm reading:

  • The Wars for Asia, 1911-1949 by S.C.M. Paine
  • The Russian Revolution: A New History by Sean McMeekin
  • The Japanese Empire: Grand Strategy from the Meiji Restoration to the Pacific War by S.C.M. Paine

I'm always curious to understand historical events through a cultural, social and strategic perspective. Much of history is represented through the dreaded "fact bubbles;" this happened, then this happened, then this happened, etc., alternatively history is Westernized or taught through the American (victor's) lense. I need to know the whole picture to understand why the world is the way it is.
